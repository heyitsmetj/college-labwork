Dussehra Festival Information

Dussehra, also known as Vijayadashami, is one of the major Hindu festivals celebrated across India. It symbolizes the triumph of good over evil and is observed on the tenth day of the bright half of the Hindu month of Ashvin.

The festival commemorates Lord Rama's victory over the demon king Ravana. In many parts of India, large effigies of Ravana are burned to symbolize this victory. The festival also marks the end of Durga Puja, celebrating the goddess Durga's victory over the demon Mahishasura.

Why Dussehra is Celebrated

Dussehra is celebrated to commemorate the victory of good over evil. It marks the day when Lord Rama defeated the demon king Ravana.

When Dussehra is Celebrated

Dussehra is celebrated on the tenth day of the bright half of the Hindu month of Ashvin, which typically falls in September or October of the Gregorian calendar.

What We Do on Dussehra

  • Attend Ramlila performances
  • Participate in or watch the burning of Ravana effigies
  • Exchange sweets and gifts with family and friends
  • Perform Durga Puja (in some regions)
  • Start new ventures or learning as it's considered an auspicious day
